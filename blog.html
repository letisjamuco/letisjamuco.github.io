Would You Give a Virtual Electric Shock to an Avatar?

29 October 2025 Reading Assignment 1

The paper Enhancing Our Lives with Immersive Virtual Reality explores many different ways in which VR can affect human behavior, from therapy and education to immersive journalism. I was especially drawn to the virtual replication of Milgram’s obedience experiment, where participants could see the avatar reacting right in front of them as they gave the “shocks.” Even though they knew it was not a real person, many still felt stress, guilt, and even tried to help by hinting at the right answers. This contrast with Milgram’s original setup, where the victim was hidden behind a wall, made me wonder whether VR can make moral conflicts feel even more real. I also found the use of VR in journalism and education interesting, because it lets people experience situations instead of just hearing about them. Still, I think it’s important to use these technologies carefully, since they can easily influence how people feel and think.

Sources

  • Slater, M., & Sanchez-Vives, M. V. (2016). Enhancing Our Lives with Immersive Virtual Reality. Frontiers in Robotics and AI .
  • Gonzalez-Franco, M., & Slater, M. (2019). Would You Give a Virtual Electric Shock to an Avatar? Scientific American Blog .
